## Understanding Additional & Hidden Fees
When checking the price of a program, always look beyond the base tuition. Be sure to ask the counselors about:
- **Application/Registration Fee:** Usually between ₹1,000 and ₹2,500, paid one-time at the time of form submission.
- **Examination Fee:** Some universities charge extra (e.g., ₹500 to ₹1,500 per semester or per paper) to handle online proctored exam software.
- **Alumni Association Fee:** A one-time charge (approx. ₹2,000–₹5,000) collected in the final semester.
- **LMS/E-Book Fee:** Usually included, but some ODL universities charge extra for printed books.

## No-Cost EMI & Bank Loans for Online MBAs
Almost all top online universities have partnered with banks and NBFCs (like Avanse, Liquiloans, and Eduvanz) to offer **No-Cost EMI plans** to students. 
- **How it works:** You pay the annual or course fees in 10 to 18 equal monthly installments.
- **Interest Rates:** 0% interest for standard tenures (usually 9–12 months).
- **Approval Time:** Approvals are completed online within 24–48 hours using basic KYC documents (PAN, Aadhaar, and salary slips).

## FAQs

### Which is the cheapest UGC-approved online MBA in India?
IGNOU (Indira Gandhi National Open University) offers the cheapest online/distance MBA in India, with total program fees of approximately ₹65,000–₹70,000. Calicut University is another affordable state government option at ~₹85,000.

### Are there early-bird discounts for online MBA admissions?
Yes. Many universities (such as Chandigarh University, Jain, and LPU) offer early-bird or session-opening discounts of 10% to 15% on the first-semester fee if you register before the early registration deadline.

### Is the fee refundable if I cancel my admission?
Refunds are governed by the strict guidelines of the UGC. Generally, you get a 100% refund (minus a minor processing fee of up to ₹1,000) if you cancel your admission before the formal commencement of the academic session.
